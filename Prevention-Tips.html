Malaria Prevention Tips

1. Use Mosquito Nets

Sleep under a long-lasting insecticide-treated mosquito net (LLIN) to reduce mosquito bites, especially at night.

2. Apply Mosquito Repellent

Use insect repellents containing DEET, picaridin, or oil of lemon eucalyptus on exposed skin and clothing.

3. Wear Protective Clothing

Cover your skin by wearing long-sleeved shirts and long pants, especially in high-risk areas.

4. Avoid Stagnant Water

Eliminate standing water around your home, as mosquitoes breed in stagnant water.

5. Indoor Residual Spraying (IRS)

Use indoor spraying with insecticides to kill mosquitoes inside homes.

6. Take Antimalarial Medication

When traveling to malaria-endemic regions, consult a healthcare professional for preventive malaria medication.

7. Keep Windows and Doors Screened

Install and maintain mosquito screens on windows and doors to prevent mosquitoes from entering your home.

8. Community Awareness

Educate yourself and others about malaria prevention and symptoms to promote early detection and treatment.
